Troubleshooting

Experiencing issues with your record player? Don't worry, we've all been there. Perfecting your vinyl listening setup can sometimes come with a few hiccups along the way, but we're here to help you get back to enjoying your music in no time!

This section is dedicated to helping you identify and resolve common problems that may arise while using your turntable. From sound issues to mechanical glitches, we've compiled a list of frequent problems and their solutions to guide you through fixing your listening experience. Please feel free to reach out via our Contact page if you need further assistance!

Common Problems

  • No sound coming from speakers

    Make sure that all cables are securely connected, the volume is turned up, and the speakers are powered on. Check if the turntable is properly connected to an amplifier or receiver if your system requires one. Also, ensure that the correct input source is selected on your amplifier or receiver.

  • Turntable is not spinning

    Ensure that the power is on and that the platter is properly seated on the spindle. If it's a belt-drive model, check if the belt is intact and correctly positioned.

  • Sound distorted or skipping

    Distortion or skipping can be caused by a dirty needle, a warped record, or improper tracking force. Make sure your needle is clean, the record is in good condition, and the tracking force is set according to the manufacturer's recommendations.

  • Humming or buzzing noise

    A humming sound can be due to grounding issues. Make sure your turntable is properly grounded, and check all cable connections to ensure they are secure. A buzzing noise may also indicate interference from other electronic devices; try moving your turntable away from such devices.

  • Tonearm moving in an incorrect fashion

    If the tonearm is not moving smoothly or is misaligned, check that it is properly balanced and that the anti-skate settings are correctly adjusted. Refer to your turntable's manual for specific instructions on balancing the tonearm.
